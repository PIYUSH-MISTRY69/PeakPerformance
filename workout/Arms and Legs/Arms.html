Alternating Dumbbell Curl

1. Starting Position: Stand upright with a dumbbell in each hand, arms fully extended at your sides, palms facing forward.

2. Curl Movement: Keep your elbows close to your body and curl one dumbbell up towards your shoulder while rotating your wrist so that your palm faces you at the top of the movement.

3. Return and Alternate: Lower the dumbbell back to the starting position and repeat with the other arm, alternating between sides for each repetition.

Incline Dumbbel Curl

1. Set Up: Sit on an incline bench set at a 45-60 degree angle, holding a dumbbell in each hand, arms fully extended, palms facing forward.

2. Curl Movement: Keeping your elbows close to your body, curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders without swinging your arms.

3. Lower Slowly: Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position in a controlled manner and repeat.

Rope Pushdown

1. Set Position: Stand facing a cable machine with a rope attachment, grip the rope with both hands, palms facing inward, elbows bent at 90 degrees.

2. Push Down: Extend your arms downward, separating the rope ends as you fully extend, targeting the triceps.

3. Controlled Return: Slowly bring your hands back up to the starting position, keeping elbows close to your body.

Cable Overhead Triceps Extension

1. Starting Position: Stand facing away from the cable machine, grasp a rope or bar attachment, and raise it overhead with your arms fully extended.

2. Extension Movement: Lower the attachment behind your head by bending at the elbows, keeping your upper arms stationary.

3. Return and Repeat: Extend your arms back to the starting position, focusing on contracting your triceps.

Dumbbell Bench Wrist Curl

1. Positioning: Sit on a flat bench with your forearms resting on your thighs, holding a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing up.

2. Curl Movement: Flex your wrists upwards, curling the dumbbells while keeping your forearms stationary on your thighs.

3. Controlled Lowering: Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position and repeat for the desired number of reps.
