Pushup

1. Start in Plank Position: Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, straighten your legs behind you, and keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.

2. Lower Your Body: Bend your elbows at a 45-degree angle, lowering your chest towards the floor while keeping your core tight.

3. Push Back Up: Press through your hands to straighten your arms and return to the starting position, keeping your body straight throughout.

Pullup

1. Grab the Bar: Grip the bar with your palms facing away, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, and hang with your arms fully extended.

2. Pull Yourself Up: Engage your back and arm muscles, pulling your chest towards the bar while keeping your body straight.

3. Lower Yourself Down: Slowly lower your body back to the starting position with controlled movement, fully extending your arms.

Bench dips

1. Position Your Hands: Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair or bench, place your hands next to your hips, fingers pointing forward, and slide your hips off the edge.

2. Lower Your Body: Bend your elbows to lower your body straight down, keeping them close to your sides until your arms form about a 90-degree angle.

3. Push Back Up: Press through your palms to straighten your arms and lift your body back up to the starting position.

Diamond Pushups

1.Hand Position: Place your hands close together under your chest, forming a diamond shape by touching your thumbs and index fingers.

2. Lower Your Body: Keep your body in a straight line and lower your chest toward your hands by bending your elbows, bringing your chest close to the diamond shape.

3. Push Back Up: Press through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position, engaging your triceps and chest.

Wide Arm Pushups

1. Hand Position: Place your hands wider than shoulder-width apart on the floor, keeping your body straight from head to heels.

2. Lower Your Body: Bend your elbows to lower your chest toward the floor, keeping your arms at a wider angle from your body than in a regular push-up.

3. Push Back Up: Press through your palms, straightening your arms to return to the starting position while keeping your core engaged.
